## Introduction from Lornette Daye

Welcome to the Finish Strong Athlete Devotional. 

As a former elite sprinter, Canadian National Team athlete, and coach, I know exactly what it feels like to stand on the starting line with the weight of the world on your shoulders. I know the sting of setbacks, the grind of daily discipline, and the pressure to perform. 

But I also know the power of resilience, faith, and mental fortitude. 

This devotional is designed for you—the student-athlete who is navigating the complexities of sports, academics, and life. Over the next 30 days, we will focus on building your identity, courage, and purpose beyond the scoreboard. My message to you is simple: no matter how the race begins, you have the power to **Finish Strong**.

Take these next 30 days to reflect, pray, and grow. You are more than an athlete. 

Finish Strong,
**Lornette Daye**

# Day 1: Identity Beyond the Scoreboard

*Romans 12:2 - 'Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind.'*

In sports, it's easy to let your stats, wins, and losses define who you are. The world will try to tell you that your worth is tied to your performance. But your true identity cannot be measured by a stopwatch or a scoreboard. You are inherently valuable just as you are. When you renew your mind to this truth, the pressure of performance begins to lift.

**Athlete Life Lesson:** Your sport is what you do, not who you are. Separate your self-worth from your athletic outcome.

**Reflection Question:** What are three things you value about yourself that have nothing to do with your sport? (Take a moment to reflect on this or write it in your personal journal).

**Prayer:** Lord, help me to see myself the way You see me. Remind me that my identity is secure in You, not in my achievements. Amen.

**Finish Strong Declaration:** I am more than an athlete. My worth is not tied to my performance. I finish strong.

# Day 2: Embracing the Pressure

*James 1:2-4 - 'Consider it pure joy, my brothers and sisters, whenever you face trials of many kinds...'*

Pressure is a privilege. It means you are in a position to do something great. Instead of running from the heavy expectations, learn to lean into them. The fire of competition is what refines your character and builds your endurance. The moments that test you the most are the moments that grow you the most.

**Athlete Life Lesson:** Pressure isn't a sign to back down; it's a signal to step up and trust your preparation.

**Reflection Question:** How do you typically react to pressure, and how can you shift your mindset to view it as an opportunity? (Take a moment to reflect on this or write it in your personal journal).

**Prayer:** God, give me the strength to stand firm under pressure. Let challenges refine me, not break me. Amen.

**Finish Strong Declaration:** I embrace the pressure. I am built for this moment. I finish strong.

# Day 3: The Power of Discipline

*Hebrews 12:11 - 'No discipline seems pleasant at the time, but painful. Later on, however, it produces a harvest of righteousness and peace...'*

Motivation will get you started, but discipline will keep you going. There will be days when you don't want to train, study, or put in the extra reps. That's when discipline has to take the wheel. The choices you make when no one is watching dictate the results everyone sees.

**Athlete Life Lesson:** Discipline is choosing what you want most over what you want right now.

**Reflection Question:** What is one area of your athletic or academic life where you need to be more disciplined? (Take a moment to reflect on this or write it in your personal journal).

**Prayer:** Lord, grant me the discipline to honor my commitments even when I lack motivation. Amen.

**Finish Strong Declaration:** I am disciplined in my daily habits. I choose long-term growth over short-term comfort. I finish strong.

# Day 4: Bouncing Back from Setbacks

*Proverbs 24:16 - 'For though the righteous fall seven times, they rise again.'*

Every athlete faces setbacks: an injury, a bad game, getting benched. The defining factor of a great athlete isn't the absence of failure, but the speed of recovery. A setback is merely a setup for a comeback if you choose to learn from it rather than sulk in it.

**Athlete Life Lesson:** Failure is an event, not a person. Rise up and try again.

**Reflection Question:** Describe a recent setback. What is one lesson you can take from it to make you stronger? (Take a moment to reflect on this or write it in your personal journal).

**Prayer:** God, when I fall, give me the courage to rise. Help me see the lesson in the loss. Amen.

**Finish Strong Declaration:** I do not stay down when I fall. I bounce back stronger. I finish strong.

# Day 5: Cultivating Confidence

*Philippians 4:13 - 'I can do all this through him who gives me strength.'*

True confidence doesn't come from a guaranteed win; it comes from knowing you've done everything in your power to prepare. It’s an inner belief that you are equipped to handle whatever happens on the field or court. When doubt creeps in, rely on the strength that is greater than your own.

**Athlete Life Lesson:** Confidence is a byproduct of preparation and faith.

**Reflection Question:** What is one specific way you can prepare better this week to increase your confidence? (Take a moment to reflect on this or write it in your personal journal).

**Prayer:** Lord, fill me with a confidence that comes from You. Silence my inner critic. Amen.

**Finish Strong Declaration:** My confidence is unshakeable because it is rooted in preparation and purpose. I finish strong.
